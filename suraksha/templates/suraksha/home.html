What is Child Abuse?

Definition of Child Abuse

Child abuse (also called child endangerment or child maltreatment) is physical, sexual, emotional and/or psychological maltreatment or neglect of a child, especially by a parent or a caregiver. Child abuse may include any act or failure to act by a parent or a caregiver that results in actual or potential wrongful harm to a child and can occur in a child's home, or in organizations, schools, or communities the child interacts with.

How Does Child Abuse Start?

Factors leading to child abuse
  • Parental Stress: Financial difficulties, lack of support.
  • Substance Abuse: Drugs and alcohol use increasing neglect.
  • History of Abuse: Cycle of abuse from past experiences.
  • Mental Health Issues: Untreated disorders affecting parenting.
  • Lack of Education: Misunderstanding of child development.
  • Social Isolation: Lack of community and support networks.

Different Categories of Child Abuse

Categories of Child Abuse
  • Physical Abuse:
    • Hitting
    • Shaking
    • Choking
    • Burning
    • Poisoning
    • Using physical restraints
  • Sexual Abuse:
    • Forcing or pressuring a child into sexual acts
    • Exposing a child to inappropriate content
    • Engaging a child in sexual conversations or behaviors
  • Emotional Abuse:
    • Rejection
    • Bullying and teasing
    • Yelling and constant criticism
    • Isolation from family and friends
    • Exposure to domestic violence
  • Neglect:
    • Failure to provide food and nutrition
    • Inadequate housing and poor living conditions
    • Lack of healthcare and hygiene
    • Insufficient supervision and safety measures

Consequences of Child Abuse

Consequences of Child Abuse
  • Physical Consequences:
    • Injuries (bruises, fractures, burns).
    • Chronic health issues and developmental delays.
  • Emotional and Psychological Consequences:
    • Depression and anxiety disorders.
    • Post-Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD).
    • Low self-esteem and feelings of worthlessness.
  • Behavioral Consequences:
    • Aggression and violent behavior.
    • Substance abuse and risky behaviors.
    • Difficulty forming healthy relationships.
  • Cognitive Consequences:
    • Learning difficulties and attention problems.
    • Poor academic performance and higher dropout rates.
  • Long-Term Consequences:
    • Persistent mental health issues into adulthood.
    • Risk of perpetuating the cycle of abuse in future generations.
